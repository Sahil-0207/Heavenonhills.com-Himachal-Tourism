Chintpurni, Una

Intro

On the border with Punjab, Una is a gateway to Himachal Pradesh. Within the state it has borders with Kangra, Hamirpur and Bilaspur. The region is a mix of both plain and hilly areas. Guru Arjun Dev, the 5th Guru of the Sikhs, is said to have named the place 'Unnati' meaning progress as a mark of respect for the hardworking people who work the land.
Una, the town is nestled on the bank of River Swan, a tributary of Sutlej. The regions low hilly terrain that gradually flattens out to merge with the plains, gives Una tropical weather which can get pretty hot in summer. The town also serves as a district headquarter.

Language Spoken:

English, Hindi and Punjabi are understood and spoken by the people associated with tourism. Locals mostly speak Punjabi, with a small section using Kangri, a dialect, in their everyday lives.

Clothing essentials:

Una with its hot climate makes cotton the best clothing choice. In winters the temperatures do fall substantially and light to heavy woollens are needed.

Destinations

Solah Singhi Dhar


The Solah Singhi Dhar, also known as the Jaswan Dhar, was the outpost of Kangra's Katoch Dynasty. It was a relatively unexplored and untouched gem. Two forts built between the 11th and 13th centuries are hidden high in the hills. They are magnificent structures even in ruins. A courtyard and a watch tower remain in the smaller fort. The larger fort, a two-story structure, is built on top of a steep cliff. The highest point on the ridge line, Piplu, provides panoramic views of Gobind Sagar Lake.
To reach the Bangana Forts one has to trek over a rocky trail. Bangana is 26 km from Una. En-route there is small temple and a well that has clean water. One must trek to the place in daytime as leopards have been sighted taking refuge in the ruins.

Shiva Temple, Ambota


The Shivbadi in Amobta village has a history dating back over 5000 years. The old Shiva Temple site is thought to have been Acharya Drona's Gurukul school, where both the Kauravas and Pandavas princes of the Mahabharata story received an early education and martial arts training. Childhood rivalries between the cousins eventually erupted into the epic battle depicted in the story. Ambota is 38 kilometres from Una and 30 kilometres from Chintpurni.

Chintpurni Temple


Chintpurni, a Shaktipeeth township, is a major pilgrimage centre that draws thousands of devotees every year. Shaktipeeth temples may be found all the way from Kashmir to Assam, and they believe that the female deity is the source of all cosmic energy.
An remarkable monument is a gold-plated shrine in the heart of the temple complex. Because vehicles are not permitted on the premises, visitors must walk to the shrine. Una town is 54 kilometres from Chintpurni.
